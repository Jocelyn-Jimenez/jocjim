Nearing Death


Role: Designer Made: Spring 2020 for Introduction to Mechanical Prototyping Team: N/A

Project Description: The final insisted we create a movable mechanism out of LEGO bricks. In comparison to previous iterations of this class, this past spring semester had a set of simple constraints for the final seeing as COVID did not allow us to work with classmates and create the traditional Sheet Metal contraption. The requirements were pretty straightforward, we had to individually create a cool, successful apparatus with LEGOs. It had to be composed of at least one CAM, a four-bar linkage, two sets of gears, and a LEGO mini figure of our choice, all within a budget of $50.


Instead of just producing a “cool” mechanism, I chose to create something that portrayed a contemplative message or story. At the beginning I was unsure of what that narrative was. After plenty of research and ideation I came up with Nearing Death.


Nearing Death emphasizes how as a society we are ruining our planet a tiny piece of trash at a time. Annually, the LEGO company manufactures approximately 20 billion elements (LEGO pieces). Of those, approximately 75 million are purchased either individually or in bulk. Like the LEGO industry, there are hundreds of other corporations that mass produce items, many of which simply turn into waste. Some thrown out by the company itself due to the lack of consumption or by the consumers after being satisfied enough with the product.


The playful structure depicts humanity watching our world gradually die due to our lack of self-control. My mini figure, Skelly, observes as the trash in the landfill/incinerator is transported to its final moments of life along with our planet. Nearing Death is made up of three main components: a massive pile of trash, an assembly line that demonstrates the burning of trash, and a moving crane claw that placed a piece of LEGO from the trash pile to the assembly line.
