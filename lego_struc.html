Nearing Death


Role: Designer Made: Spring 2020 for Introduction to Mechanical Prototyping Team: N/A

Project Description: Annually, the LEGO company makes approximately 20 billion elements. Of those, approximately 75 million are sold. Like the LEGO industry, there are hundreds of other corporations that mass produce items, many of which simply turn into waste. Some thrown out by the company itself or through the consumers. Nearing Death emphasizes how as a society we are ruining our planet a tiny piece of trash at a time.

The structure looks similar to a landfill/incinerator where a dump of trash is being moved slowly to its “final destination”. As a society we throw away hundreds of items that are simply burned or buried. Leading to toxic chemicals and gasses. My mini figure, the skeleton, is watching the trash “die” along with the rest of our planet.

Nearing Death is made up of three main components: a massive pile of trash, a moving crane claw and an assembly line that “moves” the trash from one place to another. My goal for this design was to make it as simple as possible but with a meaningful story. Therefore, there are only two main moving parts made of three mechanisms in total: a four-bar linkage, gears, and a cam.
