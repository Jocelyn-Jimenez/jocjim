UOCD with Music Educators


Role: Designer Made: Spring 2020 Team: Emma Mack, Jack Mao, James Ho, and Richard Gao

Project Description: After various hours of interviews, debriefs, persona development, and fabrication of design schematics (mind maps, empathy maps, and impact/effort matrices) we identified two insights about Music Educators (within the K-12 system): they commonly wished they had continued performing and that music was not valued in the Education System. We realized that most music educators do not pursue professional performance due to self-doubt and lack of confidence. Many decide to become educators to still fulfill their passion of music. Despite this, many still wish they performed more. After identifying the main issues, we ideated and evaluated the negative and positive consequences of each idea and chose the one that was most beneficial to all groups involved. Our solution was to address both issues by co-designing a hypothetical sabbatical program with K-12 music teachers that ensured they were given the freedom to pursue their dreams, while opening doors for college students studying music education. It also would allow them to develop networks with music departments across the nation that could potentially provide new experiences to their K-12 students.

The sabbatical program was not actually applied, only drafted and ideated. The purpose of the course was to get to learn more about our users and understand what they actually need.
